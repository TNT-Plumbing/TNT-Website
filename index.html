If you are standing near a leak or a clogged fixture, the first question is not technical. It is, "What do I do right now?" For active water, shut off the closest valve if you can do it safely. Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my water pressure low?

Low water pressure may come from a clogged fixture, partly closed valve, failing pressure regulator, pipe buildup, or a hidden leak. If it changed suddenly or affects the whole home, call a plumber.

What should I do if I have no hot water?

Check whether the water heater has power or gas and whether the temperature setting changed. If the tank is leaking, breakers trip, or the pilot will not stay lit, call TNT Plumbing.

When should I call a plumber for a leak?

Call when water is active, the source is unclear, flooring or drywall is wet, or your water bill jumps. Early leak repair can prevent larger home damage.

Can clogged drains damage my plumbing?

Yes. Recurring clogs and backups can stress pipes, expose weak seals, and send wastewater into areas where it should not be. A plumber can check whether the blockage is local or deeper in the line.
